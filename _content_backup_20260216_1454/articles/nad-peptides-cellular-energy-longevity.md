## NAD+ - The Cellular Powerhouse

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every living cell. It's essential for energy metabolism, DNA repair, and cellular signaling. Without adequate NAD+, cells can't efficiently convert nutrients into energy. As we age, NAD+ levels decline significantly - up to 50% by middle age.

This decline contributes to aging hallmarks like mitochondrial dysfunction, DNA damage accumulation, and metabolic slowdown. Factors accelerating NAD+ depletion include inflammation, oxidative stress, and excessive calorie intake.

> **Key Finding:** NAD+ decline directly correlates with aging symptoms. Restoring NAD+ levels can reverse some age-related cellular damage.

### Why NAD+ Declines With Age

Multiple mechanisms drive NAD+ depletion:

- **CD38 enzyme activation** - This NAD+-consuming enzyme increases with age
- **Impaired biosynthesis** - NAMPT enzyme activity decreases over time
- **DNA repair demands** - Aging cells require more NAD+ for DNA maintenance

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long until I feel results?

Most notice increased energy within 1-2 weeks. Full mitochondrial benefits develop over 2-3 months. Consistent use is key - NAD+ systems require sustained support.

### Can I use these peptides with NR instead of NMN?

Yes, NR converts to NMN then NAD+. Equivalent doses are 750mg NR vs 500mg NMN. Some studies suggest NMN has better bioavailability in tissues.

### Are there dietary sources of NAD+?

While foods like dairy, fish, and mushrooms contain NAD+ precursors, amounts are too low to significantly impact cellular levels. Supplementation provides therapeutic concentrations.

### What are signs of excessive NAD+ boosting?

Rare cases report temporary flushing, jitteriness, or insomnia. Reduce dosage if these occur. NAD+ optimization aims for homeostasis, not maximal elevation.

### How do peptides compare to IV NAD+ therapy?

Peptides work through biological signaling pathways rather than direct replacement. This makes them more sustainable with fewer side effects. IV NAD+ provides immediate but temporary spikes.

### Should I take NAD+ boosters continuously?

Cycling is recommended - 3 months on, 1 month off. This prevents compensatory downregulation of natural NAD+ production pathways.
