# Tesamorelin and Ipamorelin: The Growth Hormone Stack Explained

Growth hormone levels decline steadily after your late 20s. That decline correlates with increased body fat, reduced muscle mass, worse sleep, and accelerated aging.

But direct GH injection is expensive, suppresses natural production, and carries real risks at high doses. That's why peptides that stimulate your own GH release have become so attractive.

The tesamorelin and ipamorelin combination is arguably the most effective GH-stimulating stack available today.

> **Key Takeaways**
>
> - **Tesamorelin** (FDA-approved) and **ipamorelin** work through different GH-release pathways, producing synergistic results
> - The combination is particularly effective for **visceral fat reduction**, improved sleep, and better body composition
> - Both peptides **preserve natural GH pulsatility**, unlike exogenous HGH which can suppress pituitary function
> - Standard protocol: inject both **before bed on an empty stomach**, with periodic bloodwork to monitor IGF-1

## What Is Tesamorelin?

Tesamorelin is a synthetic analog of growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH). It tells your pituitary gland to produce and release growth hormone.

What makes tesamorelin special: it's the **only GH-stimulating peptide with FDA approval**. In 2010, the FDA approved it (brand name Egrifta) for reducing excess abdominal fat in HIV-positive patients with lipodystrophy ([Falutz et al., 2007](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17725375/)).

A pivotal trial showed tesamorelin reduced trunk fat by approximately **18%** over 26 weeks. A subsequent study confirmed these reductions were primarily in **visceral fat**, the dangerous deep belly fat linked to cardiovascular disease, insulin resistance, and type 2 diabetes ([Stanley et al., 2014](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24423313/)).

## What Is Ipamorelin?

Ipamorelin is a growth hormone secretagogue that stimulates GH release through the **ghrelin receptor**, a completely different pathway than GHRH.

What sets ipamorelin apart from other ghrelin mimetics (GHRP-6, GHRP-2) is its **selectivity**. It stimulates GH without significantly affecting cortisol, prolactin, or ACTH ([Raun et al., 1998](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9849822/)).

Ipamorelin keeps things clean. It also produces GH pulses that mimic natural pulsatile release patterns.

## Why This Stack Works: The Science of Synergy

> **Quick Summary:** Tesamorelin pushes the GHRH receptor. Ipamorelin pushes the ghrelin receptor. Together, GH release is greater than the sum of both.

### Two Pathways, One Goal

GH release from the pituitary is controlled by two main signals:

| Signal | Receptor | Activated By |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **GHRH** | GHRH receptor | Tesamorelin |
| **Ghrelin/GHS** | GHS receptor | Ipamorelin |

These two receptor systems **amplify each other**. When both are activated simultaneously, GH release is greater than the sum of individual effects. This has been demonstrated in research showing combined GHRH and GHS administration produced significantly higher GH levels than either alone ([Veldhuis et al., 2012](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22399510/)).

### Somatostatin Suppression

There's a third player: **somatostatin** (growth hormone-inhibiting hormone). It acts as a brake on GH release.

Ghrelin mimetics like ipamorelin partially suppress somatostatin's inhibitory effect ([Smith et al., 1997](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9360540/)). So ipamorelin doesn't just stimulate GH directly. It also removes a barrier that would otherwise dampen tesamorelin's signal.

### Preserved Pulsatility

Unlike exogenous GH injection, which creates a flat, unnatural GH profile, this stack preserves your body's natural pulsatile pattern. Peaks and troughs remain. Feedback loops still function. Your pituitary doesn't atrophy.

## Dosing Protocol

### Standard Protocol

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Tesamorelin** | 1-2 mg/day, subcutaneous |
| **Ipamorelin** | 200-300 mcg/day, subcutaneous |
| **Timing** | Both before bed, empty stomach |
| **Food restriction** | No food for 2+ hours before injection |
| **Cycle length** | 3-6 months |
| **Breaks** | 4-8 weeks off after each cycle |

### Why Before Bed?

Two reasons. GH naturally releases its largest pulse during the first phase of deep sleep. And food (especially carbohydrates) blunts GH release. Injecting fasted before bed aligns exogenous stimulus with natural peak and avoids suppression.

### Alternative: Split Dosing

| Timing | Tesamorelin | Ipamorelin |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Morning (fasted)** | -- | 100-150 mcg |
| **Before bed** | 1-2 mg | 100-150 mcg |

Works well for morning trainers who want a pre-workout GH boost. Single before-bed injection is simpler and sufficient for most people.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How quickly will I see results?

Improved sleep within the first week. Fat loss and body composition changes by **weeks 4-8**. Skin improvements around **weeks 6-12**. Full effects build over **3-6 months**.

### Can I use just one instead of stacking?

Yes. Both work individually. But the stack produces significantly more GH output. If choosing one, tesamorelin is stronger for fat loss; ipamorelin is simpler and more affordable for general GH optimization.

### Is this stack legal?

Tesamorelin is FDA-approved (Egrifta) and available by prescription. Ipamorelin is not FDA-approved but is commonly prescribed by anti-aging clinics under medical supervision. Legal status of purchasing outside clinical settings varies by jurisdiction.

### Will this suppress my natural GH production?

No. Because both peptides work by stimulating your pituitary to release its own GH, natural production pathways remain active. You're amplifying the signal, not replacing it.

### Can women use this stack?

Yes. GH optimization benefits aren't sex-specific. Women may start with slightly lower ipamorelin doses (**150-200 mcg**) and titrate up based on response.

### Can I combine this with other peptides?

Many people add [BPC-157](/articles/bpc-157-complete-guide) for gut health or injury healing, or [CJC-1295 with DAC](/articles/cjc-1295-dac-guide) as an additional GHRH analog. Keep it simple and monitor IGF-1 levels.
