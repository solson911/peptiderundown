## What Is Semax?

Semax is a synthetic peptide developed from a fragment of adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH 4-10). Russian scientists created it in the 1980s at the Institute of Molecular Genetics. This nootropic peptide enhances brain function without stimulating effects.

It's administered as a nasal spray. This allows rapid absorption into the bloodstream and brain. The peptide sequence is Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro. That's shorter than the original ACTH molecule.

> "Semax represents a novel class of neuroprotective drugs with multimodal action on the central nervous system" - *Russian Academy of Medical Sciences*

## How Semax Works: Mechanism of Action

### Brain-Derived Neurotrophic Factor (BDNF) Boost

Semax significantly increases BDNF production. BDNF is essential for neuron growth and survival. Higher levels improve synaptic plasticity. This means better communication between brain cells.

Studies show BDNF elevation within 30 minutes of administration. Effects last several hours after a single dose. Chronic use leads to sustained higher baseline levels.

### Neurotransmitter Modulation

Semax influences key neurotransmitters:

- **Serotonin**: Enhances mood regulation
- **Dopamine**: Improves motivation and focus
- **GABA**: Calms neural overexcitation

It doesn't flood the brain with chemicals. Instead, it optimizes existing pathways. This creates balanced cognitive enhancement.

### Nerve Growth Factor (NGF) Effects

Semax increases NGF synthesis in the brain. NGF supports neuron health and regeneration. This is particularly important for aging brains. Animal studies show improved nerve regeneration rates.

| BDNF upregulation | Neurogenesis, synaptic plasticity | Hours to days |
| NGF stimulation | Nerve repair, neuroprotection | Days to weeks |
| Neurotransmitter optimization | Mood balance, focus | Minutes to hours |

## Neuroprotective Effects

### Stroke Recovery

Russian clinical trials show remarkable results. Stroke patients recovered function 40% faster. Semax reduces brain tissue damage during ischemic events. It limits glutamate excitotoxicity - a major stroke damage pathway.

### Alzheimer's Protection

Research suggests preventive potential. Semax reduces amyloid-beta plaque formation. It also decreases tau protein hyperphosphorylation. Both are key Alzheimer's pathology markers.

### General Brain Protection

Semax strengthens the blood-brain barrier. It reduces oxidative stress in neural tissue. The peptide also shows anti-inflammatory effects in the CNS. These actions combine to protect against multiple damage sources.

## Dosing and Administration

### Nasal Spray Protocol

Semax is most effective as a nasal spray. Typical concentrations are 0.1% or 1%. Standard dosing protocol:

- **Morning dose**: 2-3 sprays per nostril
- **Afternoon dose**: 1-2 sprays (if needed)
- **Maximum**: 600-800 mcg daily

Start with lower doses to assess tolerance. Effects are noticeable within 15-30 minutes. The peptide has a half-life of approximately 24 minutes.

### Cycling Recommendations

Most users take Semax in cycles:

- **Standard cycle**: 2 weeks on, 1 week off
- **Intensive cycle**: 3 days on, 1 day off (max 2 months)
- **Maintenance**: 2-3 times weekly

Long-term studies show good safety up to 30 days. There's limited data beyond 3 months of continuous use.

## Side Effects and Safety

### Common Reactions

Most users experience minimal side effects. Potential temporary effects include:

- Mild nasal irritation (15% of users)
- Headache (less than 5%)
- Dizziness at high doses
- Rare insomnia if taken late

These typically resolve within 2-3 days. Reduce dosage if side effects persist. There are no reports of serious adverse events.

### Contraindications

Avoid Semax if you have:

- Acute psychotic disorders
- Severe hypertension
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Known peptide allergies

Caution is advised with MAO inhibitors. Always consult a doctor before starting. There's no evidence of dependency or withdrawal.
