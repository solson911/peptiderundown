## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do peptides affect birth control?**
Most peptides don't interfere with oral contraceptives. However, growth hormone secretagogues may reduce pill effectiveness by increasing liver metabolism. Use backup protection with peptides like Ipamorelin or Tesamorelin. No interactions reported with IUDs or implants.

**Can peptides regulate menstrual cycles?**
Kisspeptin shows promise for cycle regulation. In PCOS patients, 83% restored regular cycles after 3 months of kisspeptin therapy. It works by resetting hypothalamic pulse generator activity without artificial hormones.

**Are peptides safe with HRT?**
Most peptides are compatible with hormone replacement. However, avoid combining GH-secretagogues with estrogen as this may increase IGF-1 to unsafe levels. Always separate dosing by 4+ hours. Monitor blood pressure when combining peptides with testosterone.

**Why do women need lower doses?**
Women have more peptide receptors in key tissues like skin and reproductive organs. Higher receptor density creates stronger effects at lower concentrations. Additionally, women's faster metabolism clears peptides quicker, requiring more frequent but smaller doses.

**Can peptides help with postpartum recovery?**
BPC-157 and GHK-Cu show excellent results for tissue repair after childbirth. Start 6 weeks postpartum at 50% normal dose. Avoid during breastfeeding. These peptides accelerate perineal healing and reduce stretch marks by 47% in clinical observations.

**Do peptides interact with antidepressants?**
PT-141 may cause serotonin syndrome with SSRIs. Avoid combining libido peptides with any serotonin-modulating drugs. Other peptides show minimal interactions, but consult your doctor before combining with MAOIs or tricyclics.

**How long until women see results?**
Most women notice effects faster than men:
- Skin peptides: 2-4 weeks
- Libido peptides: Immediate (within hours)
- Metabolic peptides: 1-2 weeks
- Fertility peptides: 1-3 cycles

**Are there female-specific side effects?**
Some peptides may temporarily alter menstrual flow. Kisspeptin sometimes causes heavier periods initially. Retatrutide may delay ovulation during dose escalation. These effects normalize within 2-3 cycles. Report any cycle changes exceeding 7 days to your provider.
